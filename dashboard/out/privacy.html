Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 12, 2025

EMA Compliant Platform

This privacy policy reflects our commitment to Exoditical Moral Architecture principles: full data portability, transparent practices, and unconditional user sovereignty.

1. Introduction

Parserator ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and empowering you with complete control over your data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our intelligent data parsing services.

As pioneers of the Exoditical Moral Architecture (EMA) movement, we believe that user sovereignty and data portability are fundamental rights, not optional features.

2. EMA Principles in Practice

Your Data Rights

  • Complete Ownership: You own all data you submit to our platform
  • Full Portability: Export all your data in standard formats at any time
  • Zero Lock-in: Leave our platform with all your templates and configurations
  • Transparent Processing: Know exactly how your data is used
  • Honorable Departure: Offboarding assistance if you choose to leave

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Account Information

  • Email address and name
  • Account preferences and settings
  • Billing information (processed by Stripe)

3.2 Usage Data

  • API usage statistics and performance metrics
  • Parsing templates and configurations you create
  • Service logs for debugging and optimization

3.3 Processed Data

Data you submit for parsing is processed through our AI pipeline but is never stored permanently or used for training purposes without explicit consent.

4. How We Use Your Information

  • Provide and improve our data parsing services
  • Process billing and manage your account
  • Send service updates and technical notifications
  • Analyze usage patterns to optimize performance
  • Ensure security and prevent fraudulent activity

5. Data Processing and AI

AI Processing Transparency

Our Architect-Extractor parsing system uses Google's Gemini AI models to process your data. Here's exactly what happens:

  1. Your data is sent to Gemini for parsing (not stored by Google)
  2. Parsing results are returned to you immediately
  3. No training data is created from your submissions
  4. Processing logs are retained for 30 days for debugging only

6. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We share your information only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: Google AI for processing, Stripe for payments
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or to protect our rights
  • Business Transfers: In case of merger or acquisition (with notice)

We never sell your data to third parties. Your data sovereignty is sacred to us.

7. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Processed Data: Deleted immediately after parsing (not stored)
  • Account Data: Retained while your account is active
  • Usage Logs: Retained for 30 days for service improvement
  • Templates: Retained until you delete them or close your account

8. Your Rights and Controls

Data Portability Rights

You have the right to:

  • Export all your data in JSON, CSV, or API format
  • Download all templates and configurations
  • Request account deletion with complete data removal
  • Migrate to alternative platforms with our assistance
  • Access detailed usage reports and billing history

9. Security Measures

We protect your data using industry-standard security measures:

  • TLS encryption for all data transmission
  • API key authentication and rate limiting
  • Regular security audits and monitoring
  • SOC 2 compliance (in progress)

10. International Transfers

Your data may be processed in the United States and other countries where our service providers operate. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for international transfers.

11. Children's Privacy

Our service is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We'll notify you of significant changes via email or through our service. Your continued use after changes indicates acceptance.
